Junior College & Millennia Institute

From the 2028 JAE, JC admission shifts from L1R5 to L1R4 — one fewer subject, freeing time for broader development. HMTL plays a key role for students who take it.

🔄
Big Change: L1R5 → L1R4 for JC (from 2028 JAE)

JC eligibility threshold changes from L1R5 ≤ 20 to L1R4 ≤ 16. Students with 5 G3 subjects can now access JC — previously only available to 6-G3 students. MI threshold of L1R4 ≤ 20 is unchanged.

🌟
Higher Mother Tongue (HMTL) — Key Advantage for JC

If your child takes Higher Chinese / Higher Malay / Higher Tamil, their HMTL grade can be used as L1 if it scores better (lower points) than English Language. This can meaningfully reduce the L1R4 total. Additionally, scoring A1–C6 in both EL and HMTL earns 2 bonus points (subject to cap). HMTL is not used in polytechnic ELR2B2 calculations.

🎓 JC L1R4 Components (2028)
  • L1 — English Language or HMTL (whichever is better)
  • R1 — Best subject from Humanities
  • R2 — Best subject from Maths or Science
  • R3 — Best from Humanities, Maths or Science
  • R4 — Any 1 best-scoring subject
  • Threshold: Net L1R4 ≤ 16
📚 MI L1R4 Components (2028)
  • L1 — English Language or HMTL
  • R1 — Best from Humanities (updated 2028)
  • R2 — Best from Maths or Science (updated 2028)
  • R3 — Best from Humanities, Maths or Science
  • R4 — Any 1 best-scoring subject
  • Threshold: Net L1R4 ≤ 20 (unchanged)

Polytechnic Pathways

Two routes into polytechnic — direct Year 1 entry and the Polytechnic Foundation Programme (PFP). HMTL is not part of the poly aggregate calculation.

🆕
Poly Year 1: New Common Benchmark from 2028

All students assessed on 4 G3 + 1 G2 instead of 5 G3. The weaker B subject is mapped from G3 to G2 scale. Net ELR2B2 target: ≤ 22 points (previously ≤ 26).

📐 How ELR2B2 Is Calculated
  • EL — English Language (G3 points used)
  • R1, R2 — Two relevant subjects for your course (G3)
  • B1 — Best remaining subject (G3 points)
  • B2 — 2nd best remaining (mapped to G2 scale)
  • Subtract CCA bonus for net score. HMTL not included.
🗺️ G3 → G2 Grade Mapping
  • A1, A2, B3 at G3 → Grade 1 at G2
  • B4, C5, C6 at G3 → Grade 2 at G2
  • D7 at G3 → Grade 3 at G2
  • E8 at G3 → Grade 4 at G2
  • 9 (Fail) at G3 → Grade 5 at G2

Polytechnic Foundation Programme (PFP)

1-year programme → enters Poly Year 2. Expanded to G3 students from AY2028.

🔓
PFP Expanded to G3 Students from 2028

Now open to G3 or mixed G2+G3 students. G3 grades mapped to G2 equivalents. ELMAB3 eligibility stays at ≤ 12 points. Students enter 3 broad clusters, not a specific diploma.

ITE — Institute of Technical Education

All ITE courses upgrade to 3-Year Higher NITEC from AY2026, with expanded entry and a new guaranteed polytechnic progression route.

⬆️
All Courses → 3-Year Higher NITEC from AY2026

From AY2028, students scoring ELMAB3 ≤ 19 (at G2 level) can apply directly to Year 2 of Higher NITEC — skipping Year 1 and shortening their overall time at ITE.

📥 ITE Year 2 Direct Entry (from 2028)
  • Admission requirements set at G2 level
  • ELMAB3 ≤ 19 for Year 2 direct entry
  • G3 subjects mapped to G2 for aggregate
  • Open to G3, G2, or mixed combinations
🌉 G1 Student Bridging Pathway
  • Start from Year 1 of 3-Year Higher NITEC
  • If GPA ≥ 3.7 in Year 1 Sem 1 → bridging eligible
  • 3-month bridge → progress to Year 2 Sem 2
  • Course duration reduced to 2 years 3 months
🏆
Poly Progression Guarantee for Higher NITEC Graduates (from AY2027)

Higher NITEC graduates with GPA ≥ 3.5 are guaranteed a polytechnic diploma mapped to their ITE course. Top 10% (~1,000 graduates) guaranteed across all courses. The DPP pathway ceases from 2028. Minimum ITE→Poly GPA raised from 2.0 to 2.5 from 2027 poly intake.

📋
5th Year Eligibility

ELMAB3 computed at G2 determines 5th year eligibility. Sec 4 students (mostly G2) progressing to 5th year can upgrade to all G3 subjects if ELMAB3 ≤ 19.

ITE ELMAB3 Aggregate (from AY2028)

ELMAB3 = English Language + Any 1 Maths + Any 3 Best subjects. Computed at G2 level. Students with G3 subjects have them mapped to G2 using the grade mapping table. An ELMAB3 ≤ 19 qualifies for ITE Year 2 direct entry. Those with mostly G1 subjects enter Year 1 first.

💡 How to Read Your Score
  • For JC/MI: Lower is better. JC needs net L1R4 ≤ 16; MI needs ≤ 20
  • For Poly: Lower is better. Most courses need net ELR2B2 ≤ 22
  • For ITE: Lower is better. ELMAB3 ≤ 19 qualifies for Year 2 direct entry
  • HMTL helps for JC/MI — can serve as L1 and earn 2 bonus pts. Not used for Poly/ITE.
  • Bonus points are subtracted from raw score. JC/MI cap is 3 from 2028 (LEP is separate)
  • Always verify cut-offs on MOE CourseFinder — each course has its own MERs
